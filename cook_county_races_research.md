### Do All Municipalities Have Elected Mayors/Presidents?

**Yes.** Under the [Illinois Municipal Code (65 ILCS 5/)](https://www.ilga.gov/legislation/ILCS/details?MajorTopic=&Chapter=&ActName=Illinois+Municipal+Code.&ActID=802&ChapterID=14), every incorporated municipality in Illinois must have an elected chief executive:
- **Cities** → elected **Mayor**
- **Villages** → elected **Village President** (may also be called "mayor" by local ordinance)
- **Towns** (pre-1872) → elected **President**

Term: **4 years** (municipalities may adopt a 2-year term by ordinance)  
Election: **April Consolidated Election** (odd years)

## 2. Municipal Legislative Bodies (Suburban Cook County)

Illinois law provides multiple forms of municipal governance. The [Illinois Municipal Code](https://www.ilga.gov/legislation/ILCS/details?MajorTopic=&Chapter=&ActName=Illinois+Municipal+Code.&ActID=802&ChapterID=14) and [Mapleton Candidates Guide](https://www.mapletonillinois.com/uploads/1/6/6/9/16699010/a_candidates_guide_to_municipal_government_2021.pdf) describe the structures:

### Village Form (most suburban Cook municipalities)
- **Village President** (elected at-large, 4-year term)
- **6 Village Trustees** (elected at-large; villages under 5,000 may reduce to 4 by referendum; villages over 25,000 may elect by district)
- **Village Clerk** (elected, 4-year term)
- Village Treasurer (may be elected or appointed depending on local ordinance)

### Aldermanic-City Form (cities)
- **Mayor** (elected at-large, 4-year term)
- **Aldermen/Alderpersons** (2 per ward, 4-year staggered terms; number of wards set by ordinance)
- **City Clerk** (elected)
- **City Treasurer** (elected or appointed)

  Cities using this include: Blue Island, Berwyn, Calumet City, Palos Heights, Palos Hills, Rolling Meadows, etc.

### Commission Form (cities under 200,000 pop — rare)
- **Mayor** + **4 Commissioners** (elected, each controls a department)
- Mayor serves as Commissioner of Public Affairs

### Manager/Council Form
- **Mayor or President** + council/trustees (council hires a professional manager)
- Elected positions same as above; manager is appointed

### Chicago (special case)
- **Mayor** (elected citywide, 4-year term)
- **50 Alderpersons** (one per ward, 4-year terms; elections on last Tuesday of February in post-midterm years, with April runoff if no majority)
- **City Clerk** (elected citywide)
- **City Treasurer** (elected citywide)

### Elected Township Offices

Per [Illinois Township Code (60 ILCS 1/)](https://www.ilga.gov/legislation/ILCS/details?MajorTopic=&Chapter=&ActName=Township+Code.&ActID=770&ChapterID=13&ChapAct=60+ILCS+1%2F) and the [Township Officials of Cook County (TOCC)](https://www.tocc-il.com/divisions):

| Office | Term | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Township Supervisor** | 4 years | Chief executive; ex-officio supervisor of general assistance; has a vote on the board |
| **Township Clerk** | 4 years | Record-keeper; administers oaths; deputy clerk authority |
| **Township Assessor** | 4 years | Assesses property values; must file certificate of qualifications; 30 active in Cook County (including assessment-only Evanston) |
| **4 Township Trustees** | 4 years | Legislative body of township; together with Supervisor form 5-member board |
| **Highway Commissioner** | 4 years | Runs road district (a *separate* government); not accountable to township board; prepares and administers road district budget |

**Note on Cook County specifics:** Supervisors, clerks, and assessors are all elected in Cook County townships ([60 ILCS 1/50-10](https://www.ilga.gov/legislation/ILCS/details?MajorTopic=&Chapter=&ActName=Township+Code.&ActID=770&ChapterID=13&ChapAct=60+ILCS+1%2F)). Some townships have **multi-township assessors** where two or more small townships share one assessor position.

**Also elected:** Democratic and Republican **Township Committeepersons** (party positions, elected in primary elections, not the consolidated election).

### Township Election Calendar
- **Election:** April Consolidated Election (odd years)
- **Terms begin:** Third Monday of May following election (Supervisors, Clerks, Trustees, Highway Commissioners)
- **Assessors begin:** January 1 following election
- **Total elected positions per township:** 8 (Supervisor + Clerk + Assessor + 4 Trustees + Highway Commissioner) = **232 township positions** across 29 townships

### Elected School Board Structure

Per [Ballotpedia's Illinois school board rules](https://ballotpedia.org/Rules_governing_school_board_election_dates_and_timing_in_Illinois):

**Suburban Cook County school districts (142 districts):**
- **7-member elected boards** (standard; as of 2022, 99% of districts have 7 members)
- Some districts have **5-member boards** (grandfathered from pre-1983 law)
- **Nonpartisan** elections — no party labels
- **4-year staggered terms** (3-4 members elected every 2 years)
- Most boards elected **at-large**; 4% elected by sub-districts
- **Election:** April Consolidated Election (odd years, every 2 years)

**Chicago Public Schools (1 district — special):**
- Transitioning from appointed to elected board (2021 state law)
- **2024:** 10 members elected from 10 districts (November general election) + 10 members + board president appointed by mayor = 21-member hybrid board
- **2026:** 10 remaining districts + Board President will be elected (all 21 members eventually elected)
- **20 geographic districts** total; board president elected at-large
- **Elections:** November even-year general elections (not consolidated)
- Administered by **Chicago Board of Elections**

### Key Countywide/Regional Special Districts

**Metropolitan Water Reclamation District of Greater Chicago (MWRD)**  
- Covers all of Cook County  
- [9 commissioners elected at-large](https://mwrd.org/what-we-do/management-and-administration/metropolitan-water-reclamation-district-greater-chicago-board-commissioners), 6-year terms; 3 seats up every 2 years  
- Elections: **November even-year general elections** (aligned with state/federal cycle)  
- Administered by: both Chicago Board of Elections (Chicago voters) and Cook County Clerk (suburban voters)

**Cook County Forest Preserve District**  
- Governed by the Cook County Board of Commissioners (same 17 members + president) acting in their capacity as the Forest Preserve Board — [no separate elected board](https://fpdcc.com/about/board-commissioners/)

**Chicago Park District**  
- A special district covering the City of Chicago only
- Board of Commissioners are **appointed by the Mayor of Chicago** (not elected)

**Regional Transit (RTA, CTA, Metra, Pace)**  
- Appointed boards; no directly elected positions

### Cook County Judicial Elections

Per [Injustice Watch](https://www.injusticewatch.org/judges/judicial-elections/2022/cook-county-judicial-elections-explainer/) and the [Illinois State Board of Elections](https://www.elections.il.gov/ElectionOperations/CookSubcircuits.aspx):

- **~400 total judges** in the Circuit Court of Cook County
- **~257 circuit judges** are elected (roughly two-thirds of total)
- **~145 associate judges** are appointed (by circuit judges via secret ballot)
- Circuit judges serve **6-year terms**; must run for **retention** every 6 years (countywide ballot, 60% threshold, nonpartisan)

**Election structure:**
- **~1/3 of circuit judges** are elected **countywide** (at-large across all of Cook County)
- **~2/3 of circuit judges** are elected from **20 subcircuits** (since 2024; previously 15)
  - 6 subcircuits are entirely within Chicago (Chicago Board of Elections administers)
  - Remaining subcircuits cover suburban Cook County or span both

**Judicial elections are held:**
- **March Primary** (even years) — partisan nomination; top vote-getter from each party advances
- **November General** (even years) — partisan contest between nominees
- **November General** (even years) — **Retention elections** (nonpartisan; no opponent; need 60% yes)

```
  ├── Cook County offices: Board President, Commissioners, Sheriff, SA, etc.
  ├── Judicial: Circuit Court judges (countywide + subcircuit)
  └── MWRD: Commissioners (primary nomination)
  
NOVEMBER GENERAL (even years):
  ├── State/federal general election
  ├── Cook County offices (Board, Sheriff, SA, Clerk, Treasurer, Assessor, Board of Review)
  ├── Judicial: Circuit Court general + retention elections
  ├── MWRD: Commissioners (general election, every 2 years, 3 seats)
  └── CPS Board of Education (Chicago only, even years)

FEBRUARY + APRIL RUNOFF (odd, post-midterm — every 4 years, e.g., 2027):
  ├── City of Chicago: Mayor, City Clerk, City Treasurer, 50 Alderpersons
  └── February nonpartisan primary; April runoff if no majority winner
```

### Consolidated Primary vs. Consolidated Election

The **Consolidated Primary** (February) is required only for townships and municipalities where a **primary is needed** to select party candidates. Small townships often use a **caucus** process instead. The April **Consolidated Election** is the general election for all local offices.

For townships, primaries are required when the township has over 5,000 population **and** the boundaries are co-extensive with an incorporated area.

### Runoffs

- **Cook County (April Consolidated):** No traditional runoff system. Candidates need only a plurality.
- **Chicago municipal elections:** If no candidate receives 50%+1 in February, a runoff is held in April between the top two candidates.
- **Cook County Judicial:** No runoff — top vote-getter in primary from each party advances to November.

## 9. Cook County Clerk vs. Chicago Board of Elections

### The Split

Illinois law allows municipalities to establish their own **Municipal Election Commissions** instead of using the County Clerk for election administration. Per [Bolts Magazine](https://boltsmag.org/whats-on-the-ballot/local-election-administration/) and [Illinois State Board of Elections](https://www.elections.il.gov/ElectionOperations/ElectionAuthorities.aspx), Illinois has:
- 101 county clerks
- 1 county election commission (Peoria County)
- **6 municipal election commissions**: Chicago, Bloomington, Danville, East St. Louis, Galesburg, and Rockford

### Chicago Board of Election Commissioners
- **Jurisdiction:** The City of Chicago (all 50 wards, all of Chicago's precincts)
- **What it administers:**
  - Chicago municipal elections (aldermanic, mayoral, city clerk, city treasurer) — February/April cycle
  - Chicago's portion of state/federal primaries and general elections
  - Chicago Public Schools Board elections (November even years)
  - Chicago subcircuit judicial elections (6 subcircuits entirely within Chicago)
  - Chicago's voter registration and polling places
- **Website:** chicagoelections.gov
- **Leadership:** 3-member Board of Commissioners (appointed, not elected); Executive Director Charles Holiday, Jr.

### Cook County Clerk (Elections Division)
- **Jurisdiction:** Suburban Cook County — [more than 120 towns and villages](https://www.cookcountyclerkil.gov/elections) outside Chicago
- **What it administers:**
  - All April Consolidated elections (municipal, township, school board, park, library, fire districts) in suburban Cook County
  - All March Primary and November General elections for state/federal offices in suburban Cook County
  - Suburban Cook County judicial elections (countywide races + suburban subcircuits)
  - MWRD elections for suburban Cook County voters
  - Voter registration and polling places for suburban Cook County
  - Maintains the **Directory of Elected Officials** (2,500+ page directory covering all Cook County elected officials)
- **Elected official:** Cook County Clerk (currently Karen Yarborough)

### Data Source Implications for the Platform

| Data Need | Source for Chicago | Source for Suburban Cook |
|---|---|---|
| Municipal election results | Chicago Board of Elections | Cook County Clerk |
| Township election results | N/A (no active townships in Chicago) | Cook County Clerk |
| School board results | Chicago Board of Elections (CPS) | Cook County Clerk (143 suburban districts) |
| State/federal results | Chicago Board of Elections + ISBE | Cook County Clerk + ISBE |
| Judicial results | Chicago Board of Elections + Cook County Clerk | Cook County Clerk |
| MWRD results | Chicago Board of Elections | Cook County Clerk |
| Voter rolls / registration | Chicago Board of Elections | Cook County Clerk |

**Key insight:** Chicago and suburban Cook are **completely separate data pipelines**. The Illinois State Board of Elections (ISBE) aggregates statewide results but may not have full granular local data. For a complete Cook County platform, you need to pull from:
1. **chicagoelections.gov** (Chicago results + Chicago-specific races)
2. **cookcountyclerkil.gov** (suburban Cook results)
3. **elections.il.gov** (ISBE — statewide/state legislative/judicial aggregation)

## 11. Key Platform Design Considerations

### Jurisdictional Complexity
1. **~134 municipalities** with elected executives and legislative bodies
2. **29 townships** × 8 elected offices = **~232 township positions**
3. **143 school districts** × ~3.5 seats per election = **~500 school board seats per cycle**
4. **60+ park districts** + **40+ library districts** + **28 fire districts** = **130+ special district boards**
5. **1 MWRD** (3 seats per election, countywide)
6. **~257 elected circuit court judges** (at-large + 20 subcircuits)
7. **County-level:** ~10 offices
8. **Chicago-specific:** Mayor + 50 alderpersons + 3 citywide offices + 20-district school board

### Municipalities Straddling County Lines
~25-30 municipalities straddle Cook County's borders into DuPage, Lake, Will, Kane, and McHenry counties. For these, election results may be split between Cook County Clerk and another county's election authority. Examples: Barrington (Lake/McHenry), Buffalo Grove (Lake), Elgin (Kane), Schaumburg (DuPage), Tinley Park (Will).

### Chicago vs. Suburbs: Different Election Cycles
- **Chicago municipal:** February/April cycle (different from everything else in Illinois)
- **Suburban municipal:** April Consolidated (same as townships, schools, special districts)
- **County/state/federal:** March Primary + November General (applies everywhere)
- **CPS school board:** November even years (same as county/state/federal cycle)
- **MWRD:** November even years (same as county/state/federal cycle)

### Data Platform Recommendation
For any race that crosses the Chicago/suburban boundary (countywide judicial, MWRD, county offices), **both** the Chicago Board of Elections and Cook County Clerk results must be combined for a complete count. The Illinois State Board of Elections provides aggregated statewide data but may lag or lack granularity for local-only races.

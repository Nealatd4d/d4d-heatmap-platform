# Cook County Boundary Research
*Compiled: March 19, 2026*

---

## Overview

This document summarizes research on two Cook County district types relevant to mapping Chicago precincts:
1. **Cook County Judicial Subcircuits** — 20 total, effective December 2, 2024
2. **Cook County Board of Commissioners Districts** — 17 total, redrawn September 2021 (effective for 2022 elections)

---

## 1. Cook County Judicial Subcircuits

### Summary

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Total subcircuits (current) | **20** |
| Previous total | 15 (used through 2022/2024 elections) |
| Subcircuit numbers | **1–20** (all 20 numbers used) |
| New subcircuits added | 16, 17, 18, 19, 20 (five new) |
| Governing statute | 705 ILCS 35/2f |
| Enacted | Public Act 102-0693, signed January 7, 2022 |
| Effective for elections | December 2, 2024 (2024 general election onward) |
| Population per subcircuit | ~264,000 each |

### Legal Authority

The Judicial Circuits Districting Act of 2022 ([Public Act 102-0693](https://ilga.gov/legislation/publicacts/102/PDF/102-0693.pdf)) increased Cook County subcircuits from 15 to 20, enacted January 7, 2022. Per [705 ILCS 35/2f](https://law.justia.com/codes/illinois/chapter-705/act-705-ilcs-35/): "On and after December 2, 2024, the Circuit of Cook County is divided into 20 subcircuits as drawn by the General Assembly."

Subcircuits are required to be compact, contiguous, and substantially equal in population. Starting in 2031, they must be redrawn every 10 years following each decennial census.

## 2. Cook County Board of Commissioners Districts

### Summary

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Total districts | **17** |
| District numbers | **1–17** (all 17 numbers used) |
| Redistricting | Redrawn per Cook County Redistricting Ordinance of 2021 (adopted September 23, 2021) |
| Effective for elections | 2022 general election |
| Population per district | ~300,000 |
| Term length | 4 years |

### Legal Authority

The Cook County Board of Commissioners is governed by Section 2-109 of the Cook County Code of Ordinances. The most recent redistricting was adopted [September 23, 2021](https://www.cookcountyil.gov/service/cook-county-2020-census-redistricting) as the Cook County Redistricting Ordinance of 2021, with all 17 seats first contested under the new map in the [2022 election](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2022_Cook_County_Board_of_Commissioners_election).

There are **17 districts numbered 1–17**, each electing one commissioner.

## 4. Key Notes for Implementation

### Precinct Format
- Chicago precincts are identified as **Ward XX Precinct YYY** (e.g., "Ward 01 Precinct 001")
- Suburban Cook County precincts use a different system: **TOWNSHIP NAME + precinct number** (e.g., "BREMEN 1", "ORLAND 25")

### Data Freshness Issues
1. **Subcircuit map:** The Chicago BOE precinct report for subcircuits uses the **pre-2024 (old 15-subcircuit) boundaries**. The new 20-subcircuit map (effective Dec 2, 2024) is defined in PA 102-0693 but a Chicago BOE precinct-level report for the new 20-subcircuit map has not yet appeared at the standard URL pattern.
2. **Commissioner districts:** The March 2023 precinct report reflects the current 2021 redistricting. This should still be current as no commissioner redistricting has occurred since 2021.
3. **Ward/precinct boundaries:** Chicago wards were redistricted in May 2022 (City Council ordinance). The new ward map took effect for the 2023 election cycle. The precinct reports above use the **post-2022 ward boundaries**.
